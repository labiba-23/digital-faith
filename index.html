Introduction to the Project

In Bangladeshi perspective, religion and politics are often mixed up. Social media or streaming platforms which are the ways to socialize with people or share our perspectives, these platforms often become the tools of mobilizing or influencing the people for self agenda. In respect of the 2026 Bangladesh General Election, Islamist political party Jamaat e Islami and Bangladesh Nationalist Party(BNP) became head to head opponents. The Islamist party Jamaat’s motto was to implement Shariah(Islamic laws) as advocates of Islam to rule the government instead of secularism which is the current fundamental state policy. As a result they gained a good amount of public support as well as public backlash. This blog aims to analyze how social media shapes the ideological perspective of people to influence them, how religion comes in contact with politics as a result of public mobilization, debates over keeping secularism in the constitution or removing it and amid all these debates where does the inequality in case of gender and also the minorities actually stand. There is always a debate about removing Secularism from the constitution claiming that it clashes with the religious views of Islam which is the majority in our country. As Durkheim’s theory tells us about collective effervescence- what we all particularly feel in common about religion there is always a fear inside us that religion might get demolished. Geertz's theory also supports this idea that without this collective effort religion might come under a threat. Talal Asad terms religion as a discursive tradition as religion needs to be traced historically and at different places religion acts differently as we see different versions of it. Furthermore, we often witness clashes between different religious belief systems or views within the same religion. This phenomenon can be understood through the concept of tolerance explained by Brown, where she explained that power creates bias that disrupts the idea of tolerance. Even though Bangladeshi people preach pluralism, many times we face incidents where minority religions face disrespect and discrimination. However, Jamaat’s workforce lines up in this plural environment like ours because Islam is the most followed religion. For this, their idea of an Islamic state usually competes with secular and liberal thoughts. Fearing that religion can vanish anytime using these religious sentiments , symbols or religious talks thus it is very easy to manipulate people especially in a country like ours.We will analyze pluralism and tolerance more using the lens of Berger, Hefner, and Kaviraj. This raises a question in our mind: are we truly fighting over things that are helpful for society or are we fighting to prove ourselves right by leaving the main thing behind?
